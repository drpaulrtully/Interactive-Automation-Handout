Where AI Helps Most

Look for work that is:

High frequency, low creativity
Status emails, simple reports, scheduling, data entry.
Rules-based and precise
Data checks, reconciliations, file naming, validation.
Text-heavy but routine
Summaries, meeting minutes, follow-up emails, formatting.

Step-by-Step: Turning a Task into an AI Workflow

Step 1: Define the "Before and After"

Before: "I read 20 emails and manually draft a weekly update."
After: "AI drafts a one-page update from tagged emails; I edit and send."

Step 2: Collect 3‚Äì5 Good Examples

Use past emails, reports, or messages that represent "good" output. These guide prompts and rules.

Step 3: Design a Simple Prompt or Rule Set

Example 1: "Summarise these emails into 5 bullets for executives, highlight risks, keep under 200 words."
Example 2: "Rename files as 'Client_Project_Date_Version' based on content or metadata."

Step 4: Add a Review Checkpoint

Decide where you will always check the AI output: before sending, before filing, or before sharing with clients.

Step 5: Measure Impact

Track time saved and error rate. If it consistently saves 15‚Äì20 minutes a week, keep and refine it.
